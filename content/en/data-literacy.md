# Data Literacy

## Reading Charts and Graphs

Data visualization transforms numbers into visual stories. Understanding how to read charts is an essential skill in the information age. The most common chart types are:

- **Bar charts** — Compare quantities across categories
- **Line charts** — Show trends over time
- **Pie charts** — Display parts of a whole (best with few categories)
- **Scatter plots** — Reveal relationships between two variables

Always start by reading the title, axis labels, and legend before interpreting the data.

## Averages and Distributions

Statistics summarize data, but different measures tell different stories. The three most common measures of central tendency are:

- **Mean** (average): Sum of all values divided by the count
- **Median**: The middle value when data is sorted
- **Mode**: The most frequently occurring value

In skewed distributions — like income data where a few people earn millions — the median is often more representative than the mean. Always ask "which average?" when someone cites a statistic.

## Correlation vs. Causation

One of the most important concepts in data literacy is distinguishing correlation from causation. Two variables may move together (correlate) without one causing the other.

Classic example: Ice cream sales and drowning rates both increase in summer. Ice cream doesn't cause drowning — hot weather drives both. When evaluating claimed relationships, look for a plausible mechanism and consider hidden variables.

## Spotting Misleading Visualizations

Not all charts tell the truth. Common deceptive techniques include:

- **Truncated Y-axis**: Starting the scale above zero exaggerates differences
- **Cherry-picked timeframes**: Showing only the period that supports a narrative
- **3D effects**: Distort proportions and make accurate comparison impossible
- **Dual Y-axes**: Two different scales can make unrelated trends appear connected

When you encounter a chart, check: Does the Y-axis start at zero? Is the full context shown? Are the scales consistent?
