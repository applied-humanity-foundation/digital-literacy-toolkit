# Productivity Tools

## Cloud Documents

Cloud-based document editors have transformed how we create and collaborate. Google Docs, Microsoft 365, and Notion allow you to work from any device with an internet connection. Your documents are automatically saved, version-tracked, and backed up.

The key advantage is real-time collaboration — multiple people can edit the same document simultaneously, with changes appearing instantly. Comments and suggestion mode make review workflows seamless.

## Spreadsheet Essentials

Spreadsheets are among the most powerful everyday tools. They organize data into rows and columns, with each cell capable of holding text, numbers, or formulas.

Essential formulas to know:
- `=SUM(A1:A10)` — Adds all values in a range
- `=AVERAGE(B1:B10)` — Calculates the mean
- `=IF(A1>100, "High", "Low")` — Conditional logic
- `=VLOOKUP(value, range, col, false)` — Look up data in a table

Google Sheets and Microsoft Excel are the most widely used options, and both offer free versions.

## Cloud Storage

Cloud storage services let you store files online and access them anywhere. Most services offer a free tier:
- **Google Drive**: 15 GB free
- **OneDrive**: 5 GB free
- **Dropbox**: 2 GB free
- **iCloud**: 5 GB free

Best practices include organizing files into clear folder structures, using descriptive filenames, and setting appropriate sharing permissions.

## Collaboration Platforms

Modern teams rely on messaging platforms like Slack, Microsoft Teams, and Discord for daily communication. These tools offer organized channels, direct messaging, file sharing, and integrated video calls.

Tips for effective use: keep conversations in relevant channels, use threads for focused discussions, set your status to communicate availability, and configure notifications to avoid burnout.
